Neat NoteTaking

So, how will you participate during lectures at City?
What will you do in class?

It surprises me that some students seem to think that just sitting there will somehow give them the knowledge that they need. It doesn't - you need to think, answer questions, code and take notes. You need to engage and act and enquire to learn.

The Cornell Notes method for note-taking in lectures is worth considering.
The approach has helped some students in the past and seems useful to me.

It is explained in two short videos on the The Cornell Note Taking System. They are worth a look.

Now #CreativeCoding lectures are a little different to some other lectures, and you will have laptops out and are likely to be dealing with short introductions to self-paced activity in many cases. But taking notes like this in lectures will give you some pointers for activity and discussion in the labs, and subsequently in your own time. We do have time scheduled for discussion in the labs and through the online channels, through which we can explore the concepts, and issues introduced in the lectures.

So - take structured notes, and figure out a good way to do this so that you participate actively and effectively in lectures. How might you structure not-taking, the layout of your notes pages, for #CreativeCoding?
Your teachers and classmates can advise you - and you can likely advise them.

Inappropriate Intelligence

Of course, computers can do all sorts - they can problem solve, find information and write code for you very effectively.

OpenAI's ChatGPT is very good at writing Processing sketches as you will discover if you try it out or watch the AI at BootCamp

But we'd like you to try to complete the tasks and solve the challenges without using AI please. Doing so will give you the skills and outlook that you require to solve more complex challenges as you move through your course. We need to know that you have a grounding, a foundation in the kind of creative computing that we are focussing on here.

So let's keep the AI out of #CreativeCoding - we have plenty of use for it, and to learn about it as we go through the course, but you will only succeed in the course if you have the skills and experience that we are trying to give you in year 1. And that requires you to take the time to complete these task and exercises, think them through, get them wrong, understand them and painstakingly type the code that is required to do this.

So, once again - keep the AI out of #CreativeCoding if you want to learn the essentials of coding.

Fika Friday

Fika is 'coffee' backwards in 19th century Swedish!

  • kaffi -> ffi-ka -> fika

But it describes a tradition in which people who are often working together stop to meet, chat, exchange knowledge, opinions and work out where they are at. Usually with a coffee, but other options are available.

It is also one of my favourite cafÃ©s - Fika is a great place to stop when you are in Oakham. Call in next time you are passing through Rutland!

We are finishing #CreativeCoding with fika Friday as we want to encourage you to take time to stop, reflect and discuss your learning, your progress and your learning needs throughout your degree. We want to set up some good practice here. And we want to reward you for a full and busy two weeks and get you to discuss your experiences, your successes and failures, and ours too, with colleagues, tutors and others in the Department of Computer Science.

So please come along - we will ask you to sign up in advance so that we can cater for everybody.

We also want to give you a target - at our fika Friday we would like you to ...

  • show up - have something to eat and drink
  • meet your personal tutor - your academic guide for the next few years, somebody you need to get to know
  • identify and explain - an interesting piece of code that you could not have written at the start of #CreativeCoding and explain what it does
  • show - something that you have produced
  • see - what others have done, and chat to them. Find out how they did it, what they learned, and get them to explain - fika!
