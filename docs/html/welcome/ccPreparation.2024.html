  • https://processing.org/download/
You will be asked to do this, and given help should you need it, in the Processing Preparation class during induction.

2. Hello Processing

Spend an hour or so working your way through Dan Shiffman's impressively enthusiastic, and very useful, introduction to Processing :
  • https://hello.processing.org/

3. Dear Data

Spend an hour or so looking at the Dear Data materials that we provide and recommend.

Of course you could spend much more time on this, if it piques your interest than keep reading.

There is some guidance here to help.

7. Lecture

Use MyTimetable to find out where and when #CreativeCoding lectures and classes are.

Make sure that you have done all of the above and arrive :

  • early - latecomers are disruptive to all
  • energised - get some sleep, be ready to learn actively
  • imaginative - we need creative thinking, problem solving
  • ready - to try things out, learning through experimentation

A note on latecomers: please plan your time and activity to ensure that you are ready to learn when we start. It is distracting and disrespectful to staff and fellow students to arrive during a lecture. When we are lecturing and we see a group arrive 15 minutes into the lecture we lose concentration as we rapidly think about how we can repeat the important stuff that we have already covered to ensure that those who have just arrived do not miss out on the narrative. We plan our lectures and teaching sessions to evolve ... for the middle bits and the end to make sense, well ... you need to be there from the start!
It's really difficult when people show upo mid-lecture. So, this is not a ban on late arrivals, but a plea to you to try to avoid this in light of the disruption that you may not have considered.
Please plan to be at the lecture theatre well in advance of the start time - this is good for everybody: lecturers, students and you.
