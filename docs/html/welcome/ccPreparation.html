There is lots to do to prepare for learning in your first week at University.
Here we list 10 things that we think you need to have in hand so that you are prepared for the #CreativeCoding activity in the first week of term.

1. Processing Setup

Download and install the latest version of Processing from The Processing Foundation :
  • https://processing.org/download/
If you need a hand with this, be sure to connect with our Teaching Assistants (TAs) during one of the drop-in sessions that are scheduled in induction week.

2. Hello Processing

Spend an hour or so working your way through Dan Shiffman's impressively enthusiastic, and very useful, introduction to Processing :
  • https://hello.processing.org/

3. Dear Data

Spend an hour or two looking at the Dear Data materials that we provide and recommend.

Of course you could spend much more time on this, if it piques your interest than keep reading.
But you must complete the six tasks that we have set for you before the #CreativeCoding sessions begin in Week 01.

4. Dear Data 100

Begin thinking about your Dear Data 100 project and start some Data Collection.

There is some guidance here to help.

5. GitHub

We need to make sure that you can use GitHub for Information Management and Version Control.

Please use our guide and follow the instructions exactly so that you have the configuration that we require.

  • the Intro to GitHub

It is particularly important that you

Create a GitHub account linked to your City email address: Please do not reuse GitHub accounts that you may already have! It is essential that you (create and) use an account that is linked to your City account.

6. Moodle

You found the #CreativeCoding Homepage on Moodle - great!

Make sure that you have had a good look at these pages and checked the resources, including :

  • the Reading List
  • the Module Objectives
  • your Welcome to #CreativeCoding

7.Fib Friend

As part of the Welcome to #CreativeCoding we ask you to wear your Fib Friend badge and use it to engage with other students.

Try to find as many others as you can with the same design.
You should collect their names (perhaps in your notebook) and share something meaningful about yourself with them ... and talk about University, #CreativeCoding, your progress oin these preparation exercises and other fun stuff!

8. MyTimetable

Be sure to study your schedule on MyTimetable to find out when you need to be where.

Then schedule time around this class to begin and complete the #CreativeCoding activities.
You will need to use the time before, between and after classes to work on the concepts that we introduce in lectures and labs. You will need this time to try things out, to check documentation, watch videos, be creative, fail, succeed and learn. Be sure to use your time at Uni well.

And watch out - the timetable changes every week!

9. Lecture

Use MyTimetable to find out where and when #CreativeCoding lectures and classes are.

Make sure that you have done all of the above and arrive :

  • early - latecomers are disruptive to all.
  • energised - get some sleep, be ready to learn actively
  • imaginative - we need creative thinking, problem solving
  • ready - to try things out, learning through experimentation

Please don't chat or watch movies while in lecturers. This is distracting for everybody.
Please work to focus on the Computer Science that is being introduced, explained and discussed.
This won't always be easy - but you will need to find ways to focus, concentrate and engage in lectures to gain the knowledge and skills that you need to succeed in your degree.

10. Late

Please don't be late. Please arrive early.

Please plan your time and travel and activity to ensure that you are in the right place and ready to learn when we start.

Make sure that you are in the University at least 15 minutes before lectures start and ready to learn at 9:00am.
This means that you are seated, quiet, have your laptop charged and open, Moodle open at the correct page, and your note-taking set-up ready to go!

It is distracting and disrespectful to staff and your fellow students if you arrive during a lecture.
When we are lecturing and we see groups arrive 15 minutes into the lecture we lose concentration as we rapidly think about how we can repeat the important stuff that we have already covered to ensure that those who have just arrived do not miss out on the narrative. This is hard. We plan our lectures and teaching sessions to evolve ... for the middle bits and the end to make sense, well ... you need to be there from the start!
It's really difficult when people show up mid-lecture.
Please bear this in mind.

So, this is not a complete ban on late arrivals, but a reminder of some of the costs and a plea to you to try to avoid this in light of the disruption that you may not have considered.
Please plan to be at the lecture room well in advance of the start time - this is good for everybody:
your lecturers, your fellow students and you.
