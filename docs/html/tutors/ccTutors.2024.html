Year 1 Personal Tutorials & #CreativeCoding

We are running our new #CreativeCoding activity across term as a replacement for the #BootCamp that used to fill the first two weeks with intensive expensive fun and games with our new students.

#CreativeCoding is not a module, and is not assessed, but runs in Hijack Mode in weeks 1, 2, 7 & 8 when it uses the scheduled rooms and TAs of existing timetabled activity.

You will note Four Touch-Points when Personal Tutorials will connect with the #CreativeCoding work.

Nice NoteBooks

At the start of #CreativeCoding you will be given a nice quality notebook with graph paper pages and a couple of pens. These are to help you in #CreativeCoding and beyond.

We want you to sketch ideas and take notes as this helps you think things through, plan your learning and work things out - and you are going to need to be good at all of these things to succeed in your degree.

You also need to collaborate and communicate - get to know people and work with them. So share your coloured pens with others in the class if you need more colours. Hunt around - find somebody who has the green that you need. See if you can make a üåà through some cross-class collaboration - you'll get to meet a few of your colleagues.

Members of teaching staff will not always be right there when you need them - so make structured notes and jot down questions, reactions, ideas that you can refer to when they come by, or when they are available. Your notes can be digital or physical - but make sure that you know where they are and how to access them when the teachers are around to discuss your work with you.
