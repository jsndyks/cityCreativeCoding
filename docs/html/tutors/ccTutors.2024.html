Year 1 Personal Tutorials & #CreativeCoding

We are running our new #CreativeCoding activity across term as a replacement for the #BootCamp that used to fill the first two weeks with intensive expensive fun and games with our new students. If you know #BootCamp, it's broadly the same, but with more time and a different output.

#CreativeCoding is not a module, and is not assessed, but runs in Hijack Mode in weeks 01, 02, 07 & 08 when it uses the scheduled rooms and TAs of existing timetabled activity.

You will note Four Touch-Points when Personal Tutorials will connect with the #CreativeCoding work.

We are asking you to discuss #CreativeCoding activity with the students during these sessions across term, the data they collect and the portfolios that they make.

There is a "getting to know you" angle this year - getting to know yourself and each other through data and its creative representation - inspired by Giorgia Lupi & Stef Posavec's wonderful and inspiring Dear Data project.
The students' sketches will tell us something about their activity and interest as they collect data about themselves through term. Keen tutors may want to participate in the exercise (see below).

At the end of term you will be asked to produce a short summary of your tutees' efforts: a Creative Coding Commentary that involves a few sentences describing their initiative, participation, creativity, & group-work capability in the #CreativeCoding activity and tutorials.

This is intended to motivate students and provide a few useful reminders, that have a personal angle, for future reference writing.

As you can see, we have FOUR Personal Tutor check-ins at the start, mid and end of term. These are our pastoral sessions with a "Show Me Your SketchBook" and "Me in Data" and coding angle.

1week 01 or 02Group session to make sure that all understand the task and the tech have started data collection on an interesting theme.
2week 03 or 04Individual session to discuss sketchbook, show a in interactive method and some data.
3week 08 or 09Individual session to discuss progress, explain a sketch with an object (and a class), that data collection is progressing and to discuss the sketchbook./td>
4week 10 or 11Group session to share, compare and discuss sketches, data sets, progress and ambition.

The minimum we ask is that you connect with your tutees and book meetings as scheduled.
They should be in the same practical class group and you should be able to see this on MyTimetable and so work around it.

We recommend using the Bookings feature in Office, which works extremely well.

Tutorial 1

All you need to do in weeks 01 or 02 is say Hello, do some introductions and ask what they need to do in #CreativeCoding.

They should be trying to come up with a Dear Data Diary ... collecting data about their lives that is based around some topic:

  • the topic must be original and unique - they have been given some creativity techniques, get them to explain and use these
  • the topic must be something that interests and / or is meaningful to them - ask them why it matters! Get them to discuss this and what they are collecting about the topic - and how it is going!
  • the data must not reveal identifying personal information - be anonymous you can advise on this and discuss potential for identity leak

If you are unable to answer anything, or receive questions about #CreativeCoding, then get them to me in an email with CC24 in the subject field please.

  • encourage students to get to know themselves and their peers through data, to reflect on their relationship with data
  • develop the Y1 Computer Science community
  • learn some core technology for productivity & collaboration and practice
  • build relationships between students and tutors
