Year 1 Personal Tutorials & #CreativeCoding

We are running our new #CreativeCoding activity across term for the Year 1 CS students. It replaces the #BootCamp that used to fill the first two weeks with intensive expensive fun and games. If you know #BootCamp, it's broadly the same, with re-appropriated material, but with more time and a different output.

#CreativeCoding is not a module, and is not assessed, but runs in Hijack Mode in weeks 01, 02, 07 & 08 when it uses the scheduled rooms and TAs of existing timetabled activity. It is also the focus for the Year 1 Personal Tutorials and this is an attempt to brief Personal Tutors on how this works and what is required.

You will note Four Touch-Points when Personal Tutorials will connect with the #CreativeCoding work.

We are asking you to discuss #CreativeCoding activity with the students during these sessions across term, the data they collect and the portfolios that they make.

There is a "getting to know you" angle this year - getting to know yourself and each other through data and its creative representation - inspired by Giorgia Lupi & Stef Posavec's wonderful and inspiring Dear Data project.
The students' sketches will tell us something about their activity and interest as they collect data about themselves through term. Keen tutors may want to participate in the exercise (see below).

At the end of term you will be asked to produce a short summary of your tutees' efforts: a Creative Coding Commentary that involves a few sentences describing their initiative, participation, creativity, & group-work capability in the #CreativeCoding activity and tutorials.

This is intended to motivate students and provide a few useful reminders, that have a personal angle, for future reference writing.

The minimum we ask is that you connect with your tutees and book meetings as scheduled.
They should be in the same practical class group and you should be able to see this on MyTimetable and so work around it.

We recommend using the Bookings feature in Office, which works extremely well.

We suggest that you book all of the tutorials right away and get them in your diary and the students' diaries.

Tutorial 1

All you need to do in weeks 01 or 02 is say Hello, do some introductions and ask the students what they need to do in #CreativeCoding. Let them explain their learning.

They should be trying to come up with a Dear Data Diary ... collecting data about their lives that is based around some topic:

  • the topic must be original and unique - they have been given some creativity techniques, get them to explain and use these
  • the topic must be something that interests and / or is meaningful to them - ask them why it matters! Get them to discuss this and what they are collecting about the topic - and how it is going!
  • the data must not reveal identifying personal information - be anonymous you can advise on this and discuss potential for identity leak

Please help them make a judgment here - pretty much anything goes!
Ask them to explain why this is meaningful to them, why it is likely to be original and to reassure you about steps taken to ensure anonymity.

If you are unable to answer anything, or receive questions about #CreativeCoding, then get them to me in an email with CC24 in the subject field please.

And what is the point of the #CreativeCoding / Personal Tutorial crossover? Broadly ...

  • to build relationships between students and tutors
    letting us get to know them, letting them get to know us - around some self-identified theme(s)
  • to generate evidence of their capabilities for when we need to write references for employers or further study
    this is intended to be an efficient way of establishing and remembering who the students are and giving us something to go on at reference time
  • to reframe concepts of data a little and have some fun
    discussing portfolios of sketches with creative approaches to data collection and representation might actually be interesting, thought provoking and enjoyable.

You also need to know a little about their #CreativeCoding task and the Dear Data Diaries that they will be developing, showing and sharing.
Invest a little time in these three short resources that collectively involve about 15 minutes of reading & watching.

  • The Dear Data Introduction - the start of the book explains the project succinctly and we've digitised it so it's accessible online to all
  • The to DRAW is to REMEMBER annotated guidance from the book that persuades us that "IT'S not THAT HARD" to participate in a Dear Data project
  • The short (3 min) Dear Data (Big Bang Data) video that outlines the original project - useful context

Incidentally, the TALIS Reading Lists that we have associated with each module work superbly and are an excellent and efficient resource. Selecting, annotating and prioritising resources seems to me to be absolutely core academic activity, that is extremely useful for students. Letting them know what they should read, and why, and giving them access to it is a fantastic way of getting our knowledge to them. If you don't produce annotated, ordered reading lists, then I strongly encourage you to do so - it works extremely well and the library provide great support.

Well, if you can do all of that, you might as well collect some personal data yourself and make a visualization. Discuss it with your students - what does it reveal. Be creative. Be inspiring! Reveal yourself in data with your students and compare your experiences and discoveries.
