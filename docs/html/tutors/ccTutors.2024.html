Year 1 Personal Tutorials & #CreativeCoding

We are running our new #CreativeCoding activity across term as a replacement for the #BootCamp that used to fill the first two weeks with intensive expensive fun and games with our new students. If you know #BootCamp, it's broadly the same, but with more time and a different output.

#CreativeCoding is not a module, and is not assessed, but runs in Hijack Mode in weeks 01, 02, 07 & 08 when it uses the scheduled rooms and TAs of existing timetabled activity.

You will note Four Touch-Points when Personal Tutorials will connect with the #CreativeCoding work.

We are asking you to discuss #CreativeCoding activity with the students during these sessions across term, the data they collect and the portfolios that they make.

At the end of term you will be asked to produce a short summary of their efforts: a Creative Coding Commentary that involves a few sentences describing their initiative, participation, creativity, & group-work capability in the #CreativeCoding activity and tutorials.

This is intended to motivate students and provide a few useful reminders, that have a personal angle, for future reference writing.

As you can see, we have FOUR Personal Tutor check-ins at the start, mid and end of term. These are our pastoral sessions with a "Show Me Your SketchBook" and "Me in Data" and coding angle.

1week 01 or 02Group session in Week 01 to make sure that all understand the task and the tech have started data collection on an interesting theme.
2week 03 or 04Individual session in Week 03 to discuss sketchbook, show a in interactive method and some data.
3week 08 or 09Individual session in Week 08 to discuss progress, explain a sketch with an object (and a class), that data collection is progressing and to discuss the sketchbook.
4week 10 or 11Group session to share, compare and discuss sketches, data sets, progress and ambition.

One thing that will probably kill your interest is a set of learning objectives, but don't scarper just yet, we need to know collectively what the point is, and we have tried to keep it simple.

Specifically, we hope to use #CreativeCoding and the associated tutorials to ...

  • introduce ways of learning at University - experimental, autonomous
  • introduce key programming concepts
  • encourage creative thinking
  • encourage computational thinking
  • encourage students to get to know themselves and their peers through data, to reflect on their relationship with data
  • develop the Y1 Computer Science community
  • learn some core technology for productivity & collaboration and practice
  • build relationships between students and tutors
