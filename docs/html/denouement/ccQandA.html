Show Me A Solution Questions ...

» I am confused on how to do my project, it would be helpful to have some sort of example of how the project it to be layed out

It would be helpful, and we find that students are excellent at reproducing examples that we give them.
But this is not what employers want and it's not what students find that they need when they have left Uni.

So #CreativeCoding takes a different approach.
We find that model solutions are constraining and we want to jolt you out of this culture of copy.
Thinking is hard! But degrees are hard sometimes and we want to give you the time, space, techniques and opportunity to practice original thinking.
#CreativeCoding gives you the chance to design a solution to a problem that is specified without a model answer.
Many real-life design challenges are like this.
Designing #CreativeCoding was like this - we didn't have a model module to base this activity oin, just some ideas about what we wanted to achieve and how we might achieve it. We had to be creative in our thinking to come up with a viable (hopefully) solution. And this is it!
We will ask for feedback to help improve our design before too long!

We explain this, and the Design Process that we would like you to take in the Creativity : Core notes - see Section 3. Creative (Dear Data 100) Design Process ....
And also have a look at the short and helpful Dear Data - To DRAW is to REMEMBER annotated pages that explain why "It's not that hard" to do a Dear Data diary.

And ... when you think about it ... the whole body of #CreativeCoding materials is packed full of examples!
Be creative with them.

Persuade Me To Do It Questions ...

» What is the level of work expected of us to submit?
Hmmmm ...
Well, you set the level. Don't worry about anybody else - why do they matter?
Try to do something that you are pleased with.
Show us something that you can do now that you were not able to do in September. We want to see evidence of learning and creativity.
The only way to fail #CreativeCoding is to disengage and not submit anything - this would be a great shame.
We are trying to make this as stress-free and fun as possible - you have nothing to lose and lots to learn!
Don't let any worrying about levels stop you from participating.
» How interactive / creative does the data need to be presented?
How do we answer this one?!
As creative and expressive as you want it to be!
Try using SCAMPER and a few design experiments to play with the way the data are depicted.
Do your best, try to produce something that makes you happy, do something that you were unable to do in September.
» What if we can't find a way to get to 100 individual data pieces?

Hmmmm ...
OK - I'm going to offer some tough love here - we need to give this sometimes! ‚ù§Ô∏è

I don't think you are being sufficiently creative in your data collection thinking!
We all observe hundreds, thousands, millions of events every day ... it's just a matter of :

  • noticing;
  • deciding which to record;
  • deciding what to record about them

So go for it!

In class I noticed the interesting wooden floors in The Great Hall and suggested recording the different surfaces that I put my feet on throughout the day. I could record the time (day number, hour, minute), the colour, the soft/hardness of the surface and take a photo.

I also suggested recording the sequence of buttons that I pressed during a game of Guitar Hero.

You could record colour (text) and whether they were right or wrong (boolean). You could get a friend to record for you in real time, or video yourself and play back in slow motion later. You could add the video to the sketch with the Processing video capability, like the Movie class. See Sound and Video : Core.

My favourite guitar controller has seen me totally kill 'One' a few times when I have a Guitar Hero Metallica session (on easy mode).

But don't do these things - do something that is inspired by them and Dear Data.
Use SCAMPER to Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify ...

  • A different game;
  • The floor surface you are on when somebody expressed gratitude;
  • Multiple efforts at the same game, multiple people playing the same track / game;
  • The floor surface you are on at 12:00;
  • Etc.

And do try the short and helpful Dear Data - To DRAW is to REMEMBER annotated pages that explain why "It's not that hard" to start a Dear Data diary.

» Does our program have to include all 100 observations i.e having 100 circles or can I group them?

Interesting!
We hope that you will show the items individually ... otherwise you could have a single rectangle representing 100 items and simply say that this is my data. That would not be too exciting or expressive.

Most of the Dear Data Weeks show many items and we'd like you to do the same. This kind of detailed depiction gives you the best chance of making an interesting discovery.

We would also encourage you to collect plenty of data for each observation, as once again more detail gives you more possibilities for expressive depiction and interesting discovery.

Submission Details

The submission instructions below are taken from the Dear Data 100 Demo (Assignment Details & Submission) .
Load the page and click Add submission.

Your submission must contain uploaded files and online text as follows ...

Online Text

Please type two things into the text box:

  1. first add the URL that enables us to locate and download the sketch from GitHub
  2. then add a brief textual description that lets us know what we are looking at and whether we need to do anything to interact with it

Uploaded Files

Please upload two more files (so the submission has four components in total) :

  1. an image showing what your Dear Data sketch looks like
    (JPEG or PNG please, use 'save()' in Processing or a screen dump and then upload the image).
  2. a zip file containing the sketch folder (and its contents!) and any data that you use
    (most operating systems will allow you to zip up a folder when you _right click_ on the folder icon in a file manager).

Be sure to submit these four items exactly as specified here, well before the submission deadline.
