Dear Data 100

We want you to collect data in similar fashion to the Dear Data Project over the term.

We will show you how to use Processing to create a Dear Data 100 demo that explores some aspect of the World that is interesting to you, using your code to engage with your data (and vice versa) and using code to express your data in ways that are meaningful to you.

With luck, you will learn about yourself and some aspects of the world as you embark upon some discovery by depicting your data in creative ways.

The task, to be completed by the end of term, with our support, is open ended, and broadly specified - all we ask is that ...

Over this term, as part of the #CreativeCoding activity you ...

Create a Processing sketch
that depicts a data set that you have collected over time
that explores some aspect of the World
that is interesting to you
through at least 100 observations

Hopefully ...

in creative & expressive ways
that you are pleased with and
that helps you learn (a little) about yourself & The World around you

Choose Some Topics

So, where to start?
You will need to do a little reading and some thinking.
But that's great because universities are fantastic places to read and think.
It's pretty much what they are set up for! (plus some listening).

The Topic and Data that you collect must be:

  • ORIGINAL - the topic should be original - use the creativity techniques that we have touched upon, like SCAMPER to make it as uniquer as you can;
  • MEANINGFUL - the topic must be something that interests and is perhaps important to you;
  • ANONYMOUS - the data must not reveal identifying personal information;
  • NUMEROUS - you must collect a minimum of 100 observations to show in your demo;

So have a think.
What could you do?
What are you interested in?
What could you collect?
What might work for you?

Task 4 : Dear Data 100 (preliminary thinking)

Have a look at the guidance provided at the bottom of the Dear Data And FiveThirtyEight Want You To Visualize Your Podcast Habits page.

Begin to think about the data that you might want to collect - and see whether this is feasible.
Practice, try a few things out ...

  • is this interesting?
  • is it feasible?
  • does it work?
  • might it be ORIGINAL - MEANINGFUL - ANONYMOUS - NUMEROUS?

Then ask yourself the questions that Giorgia & Stephanie ask:

  • What do you want to know and explore? - What is your main question?
  • What detail and context do you need to record? - What are the additional questions?

Try to find two or three topics and think about which questions you could ask and what you could record. And how.
Discuss them with others - do they seem feasible? Interesting? Motivating?
Are they ORIGINAL - MEANINGFUL - ANONYMOUS and do they have the potential to be NUMEROUS?
