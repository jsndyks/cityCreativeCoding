Three short tasks will introduce you to the Dear Data Project, which will provide a focus for our #CreativeCoding this term.

Please complete them before the first lecture.

Postcards

We are going to ask you to collect data.
Data about yourselves.
Or some aspect of The World that interests you.
And we are going to broadly follow a model introduced in the Dear Data Project.

10 years ago - two amazing Women, one in London, one in New York, embarked on a data sharing experiment. Giorgia & Stefanie collected personal data about a different theme each week over the course of a year.
They presented aspects of their data, to explore the theme, on a postcard.
They then posted the cards to each other on opposite sides of The Atlantic.

This is all nicely summarised in the short Dear Data (Big Bang Data) video that we recommend.

Task 1 : Postcards
Please watch the short Dear Data (Big Bang Data) video.
It's an informative 3 minutes that will introduce the project to you nicely!

The Book

The project was published online and in a book Dear Data.

It emphasises the notion of Data as a starting point for questioning and understanding the world around us, rather than data as the definitive answer to all of our questions.

Dear Data is entertaining, revealing and inspiring enough that we want to use some of its ideas in learning about ourselves through data - learning about the relationships between code, data, ourselves and each other.

Dear Data is also available for loan from the library for those who are interested - see the Creative Coding Reading List - selected sections are available online to everybody.

For now it is important that you read the Introduction in preparation for #CreativeCoding.

Task 2 : The Book (Introduction)
Read the Introduction to Dear Data to understand the project.
This is important context for the Dear Data Diary task that you will be set in #CreativeCoding.

Creative Material

We are excited by the approach that Giorgia and Stefanie have taken.

They see data as creative material that can be a starting point for play and expression as we begin to find ourselves, using data and its depiction as a new way of seeing and engaging with The World.

We will try to use the #CreativeCoding activities to inspire you to do the same.
