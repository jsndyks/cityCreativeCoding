Postcards

We are going to ask you to collect data.
Data about yourselves.
Or some aspect of The World that interests you.
And we are going to broadly follow a model introduced in the Dear Data project.

10 years ago - two amazing Women, one in London, one in New York, embarked on a data sharing experiment. Giorgia & Stefanie collected personal data about a different theme each week over the course of a year.
They presented aspects of their data, to explore the theme, on a postcard.
They then posted the cards to each other on opposite sides of The Atlantic.

Giorgia & Stefanie post a weekly Dear Data visualization

The Book

The project was published online and in a book and emphasises the notion of Data as a starting point for questioning and understanding the world around us, rather than data as the definitive answer to all of our questions.

Dear Data is entertaining, revealing and inspiring enough that we want to use some of its ideas in learning about ourselves through data - learning about the relationships between code, data, ourselves and each other.

Creative Material

We love the approach that Giorgia and Stefanie have taken.

They see data as creative material that can be a starting point for play and expression as we begin to find ourselves, using data nad its depiction as a new way of seeing and engaging with The World.

We will try to use the #CreativeCoding activities to inspire you to do the same.
