One thing we have not emphasized sufficiently:

It is easy to have an IDEA, to know what your INTENTION is for the script, but to not be able to write the CODE to do it.

This can be frustrating with lots of blockages stopping you from getting to where you had hoped to arrive.

We don't have the time or energy to bang our head against frustrating blockages.

BootCamp and Processing work best if you have an idea, see what happens when you code, and go where the code takes you!

Try things out, follow your nose, and develop your ideas as to where you are going and what you are going to achieve - and make the most of what you learn on the way.

You might end up nowhere near where you thought you were going, but you may get somewhere interesting and learn a lot.

Take this attitude into your final afternoon of BootCamp coding as you work on the demos.

And get plenty of sleep tonight, to make the most of tomorrow

Some people think this is a good attitude for life, and perhaps for University.
But that's for you to decide.
