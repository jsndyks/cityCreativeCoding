Fika is (apparently, supposedly) 'coffee' backwards in 19th century Swedish!

  • kaffi -> ffi-ka -> fika

It describes a tradition in which people who are often working together stop to meet, chat, exchange knowledge, opinions and work out where they are at. Usually with a coffee, but other options are available. Sounds nice, right?

We are finishing Coding BootCamp, our two week intensive introduction to creative coding that we impose upon all of our fresh CS undergraduates with fika Friday as we want to encourage students to take time to stop, reflect and discuss their learning, their progress and their learning needs as they start their degrees. We want to set up some good practice here. And we want to reward students and staff for a full and busy two weeks and get them to discuss their experiences, their successes and their failures, and ours too, with colleagues, tutors and others in the Department of Computer Science.

So please come along - we need you there.

We need you to find and engage with your tutees.

We gave students a target - at our fika Friday we would like them to ...

  • show up - have something to eat and drink
  • meet you - their personal tutor - your are their academic guide for the next few years, somebody they need to get to know
  • identify and explain - an interesting piece of code that they could not have written at the start of #BootCamp and explain what it does
  • show - something that they have produced
  • see - what others have done, and chat to them. Find out how they did it, what they learned, and get them to explain - fika!

You are a big part of this. Please play your role. We have ordered coffee and some breakfast for you.
